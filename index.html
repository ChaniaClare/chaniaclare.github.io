About Me

I'm a final-year PhD candidate at University College London, specializing in computational systems and synthetic biology. My research focuses on understanding microbial community dynamics through mathematical modeling and machine learning approaches.

With a unique combination of wet lab expertise and computational skills, I bridge the gap between biological discovery and data-driven insights. My experience spans from working with hazard level 2 & 3 bacteria to developing machine learning models for antimicrobial resistance prediction.

I'm passionate about translating complex biological systems into actionable insights for healthcare and biotechnology applications. Currently seeking opportunities in biomedical data science where I can apply my interdisciplinary background to solve real-world problems.
